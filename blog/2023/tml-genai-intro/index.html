Introduction

Who behind the wheel?

The wheel? The wheel is the decision making process. The closest analogy is the autopilot system in the Tesla car, which fully controls the car. However, there are also other examples of The wheel where it seems that the human is still in control, but in fact, the AI/ML system is significantly influencing the decision making process. And it can be seen in every daily life activities, such as

  • Buying a product on Amazon: You only see the products that are recommended or listed by the ML system.
  • Searching on Google: What you see is the result of the ranking algorithm.
  • Going from A to B: The route is recommended by the navigation system.

So we live in a world where AI/ML is everywhere, and it is influencing our decision making process. There are few activities that are still under the control of human, such as, doctor’s diagnosis, judge’s decision, etc. However, sooner or later, more and more decisions will be made by AI systems.

If AI is behind the wheel, can we trust it?

Untill now, the most advanced autonomous car is still at level 2, which means that we still need a wheel and a driver to take over the control when the system fails. Or doctors still need to make the final decision on the diagnosis. It is the life and death decision, which requires the highest level of trust. However, with less critical decisions, such as buying a product, searching for information, etc., we are already trusting the AI system. We use their recommendations without questioning. So what is the reason behind this trust?

  • The system brings us benefits or convenient life. We even sacrifice our privacy for the convenience, such as using voice assistant system, etc.
  • Sometimes, we don’t have a choice. For example, less and less people are using paper map to navigate. We have to use the navigation system. Think about the situation when in the future, people don’t know how to drive a car.
  • It is developed by a big company, such as Google, Facebook, Amazon, etc. So we can trust it.
  • The system is transparent. We can understand how the system works, and we can verify the result . No, we can’t.

Because of the above reasons, in this stage, we - the users of the AI systems - are forced to trust or have to trust the system. And the developers, have less incentive to make the system trustworthy. (Explaining why Adversarial Machine Learning is not a hot topic in the industry). Building a trustworthy system is also hard, expensive, and reduce the performance of the system on non-adversarial data, that is another reason why the developers don’t want to do it.

So, if it is, why do we need to build a trustworthy system? Why do we need to care about it? You might also see that more and more companies talk about their effort on building trustworthy AI systems. Why?

  • Because we sacrifice our privacy for the convenience, untill something bad happens, and actually, it already happened. It raises the awareness of the users about not only the privacy, but also the trustworthiness of the system.
  • Because we have more choices. Google search is being challenged by Bing, DuckDuckGo, etc. Amazon is being challenged by Alibaba, etc. So the users have more choices, and they can choose the system that they trust.

So, after all, I hope that you can agree with me on the importance of building a trustworthy AI system.

Trustworthy Machine Learning

What is a trustworthy person? You can see that even with this simple question, we can’t have a clear answer. So trustworhthy machine learning is a similar difficulty level question. Common accepted definition of trustworthy machine learning is based on its properties, such as explainable, fair, privacy-preserving, causal, and robust (ref. Trustworthy ML Initiative).

Another approach is to narrow down the scope of the problem, as in Unsolved Problems in ML Safety which focuses on the safety of the ML system with four problems: robustness, monitoring, alignment, and systemic safety.
